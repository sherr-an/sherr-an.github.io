About me

Hi there, my name is Xinyue (Sherry) An, and I am a first-year Neuroscience PhD student. I have always been intersted in unknown, mysterior things, especially the brain that sits on top of our body as the core of intelligence. Starting my undergrad as a Biology major (and Pre-Med for one semester or so), I later shifted gear toward Neuroscience and found my passion in the elegant fusion between Neuroscience and Computer Science - the studies of the two potent intelligent systems. Because one shall only use a diamond to cut another diamond, to me, artificial intelligence could be the best tool to interpret natural intelligence. You may argue that the study of brain, using brain, would also do the job. This is my favorite recursion, and definitely interesting to think that our brain helps us hack itself.

Outside of lab, I like to make espresso drinks and cocktails - one to sober, the other to numb. Not a master yet but enough to make myself happy. I also enjoy music, podcast, reading, and hiking.
